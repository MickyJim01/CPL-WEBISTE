Privelages and Limitations

"The holder of a commercial pilot licence is authorised to pilot, as pilot in command, any aircraft in any operation" -CASR Part 61.570

Privelages

With a commercial licence, a pilot can conduct commercial operations and recieve remuneration.

However, this is subject to some exceptions:

  • A commercial pilot cannot be PIC in Multi-Crew Operations and;
  • May not be PIC of an aircraft certified for single pilot operations, that weighs more than 5700kg, with less than 750 hours,

Limitations

Requirements for the Grant of the Licence

Must be 18 years old

Must have passed all required exams

Must have conducted the required training

Must pass the flight test

Must meet the minimum hour requirements:

  • 200hrs Aeronautical Experience
  • 190hrs Actual Flight
  • 100hrs PIC
  • 20hrs Cross Country
    • At least 1 XC flight must be >300nm and include 2 T/O & LDG's
      that are not at the departure aerodrome
  • 10hrs Instrument
    • 5hrs must be in actual flight

General Limitations

Must have appropriateCategory Rating

Types of Categories
  • Aeroplane
  • Helicopter
  • Gyroplane
  • Powered Lift
  • Airship

Must have appropriate Class Rating

Types of Class
  • Single Engine
  • Multi Engine

Must have appropriate Type Rating

Prescribed by CASA when an aircraft requires additional training to be flown safely due to:
  • Complex Systems or;
  • Performance/Handling Characteristics
All Multi Crew certified A/C & some Single pilot A/C require Type Ratings

Must have appropriate Design Feature Endorsement/Flight Activity Endorsement

Design Features:
  • Tailwheel
  • Rectractable Undercarriage
  • MPPC
  • GTE
  • Multi-Engine Centreline Thrust
  • Pressurisation
  • Float Plane
  • Floating Hull
  • Ski Landing Gear
Flight Activity
  • Aerobatics

Must be Generally Competent

Be Familiar With;
  • Navigation Systems
  • Normal, Abnormal, and Emergency Procedures
  • Operating Limitations
  • Weight and Balance
  • Performance

Must have appropriate Operational Rating

Op Ratings Include:
  • Instrument Flight Rating
  • Low Level Rating
  • Night VFR Rating
  • Aerial Application Rating
  • Flight Instructor/Examiner Rating

Must be Recent

To Carry PAX:
  • 3 T/O & LDG's in the last 90 days
  • T/O must be followed by a climb to 500'

Must have the appropriate Medical Certificate

For Commercial OPs - Class 1

Must carry all appropriate Documents

  • Flight Crew Licence
  • Medical Certificate
  • Photo ID <10yrs old

Must have current AELP assessment

Not required if operating in Australian territory only

Must have a valid Flight Review

  • Must be done every 24 months
  • Must be done by Grade 2 instructors or Grade 3 with FIR, CLR, & SEA endorsements
  • For low level and night flying, instructors must have relevant endorsement

Must have a valid Proficiency Check

  • Must be done every year
  • Valid for 1 year to the end of the month after the month in which the check was conducted
  • Must be conducted by flight examiners or others certified by CASA
